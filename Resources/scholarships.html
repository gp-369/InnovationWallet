Scholarships for Minorities and Young People

Pursuing higher education can be expensive, but scholarships can alleviate the financial burden. Below, you will find scholarships tailored specifically for minority groups and young people, including detailed information on eligibility, application requirements, and deadlines.

Top Scholarships You Should Apply For

  • Gates Millennium Scholars Program
    Eligibility: Open to outstanding minority students who demonstrate financial need and a commitment to leadership and service.
    Details: Covers tuition, fees, room, board, and books for undergraduate and graduate education. Requires a minimum GPA of 3.3 and participation in community service.
    Deadline: January 15, 2024
    Learn More
  • Hispanic Scholarship Fund
    Eligibility: Hispanic/Latino students with a minimum GPA of 3.0. Must plan to enroll full-time in an accredited college or university.
    Details: Awards range from $500 to $5,000. Focuses on students in STEM fields.
    Deadline: February 1, 2024
    Learn More
  • Asian & Pacific Islander American Scholarship Fund (APIASF)
    Eligibility: Asian and Pacific Islander students who are first-generation college students and demonstrate financial need.
    Details: Provides up to $20,000 for college expenses. Special consideration for students from underserved communities.
    Deadline: January 31, 2024
    Learn More
  • Jackie Robinson Foundation Scholarship
    Eligibility: Minority high school seniors with leadership skills and a commitment to community service.
    Details: Provides up to $30,000 over four years, along with mentoring and leadership opportunities.
    Deadline: February 15, 2024
    Learn More

Tips for Winning Scholarships

  1. Start Early: Research scholarships as soon as possible to avoid missing deadlines.
  2. Tailor Your Application: Personalize your essay and application to align with the mission of the scholarship.
  3. Seek Recommendations: Ask teachers, mentors, or employers to write strong letters of recommendation.
  4. Proofread: Ensure your application is free of grammatical errors and typos.
  5. Apply to Multiple Scholarships: Increase your chances by applying to as many scholarships as you qualify for.
