Introduction to Credit

Credit is an agreement with a lender and a borrower that allows the borrower to purchase goods or services now, with the promise of paying the lender back, usually with interest.

Types of Credit

  • Revolving Credit: A type of open credit card that's automatically renewed as you pay off your debts.
  • Installment Credit: A type of loan where you borrow a lump sum and pay it back in fixed amounts monthly.
  • Secured Credit Cards: Backed by a cash deposit from the cardholder.
  • Student Credit Cards: Designed for students with lower credit limits and interest rates.
  • Rewards Credit Cards: Offer rewards for purchases at participating stores.
  • Store Credit Cards: Issued by retailers and brands for their customers.

How Credit Scores Work

Credit scores predict how likely someone is to repay a loan and make payments on time. They range from 300 to 850 and are calculated using credit report data.

Scores are usually determined by these 7 points:

  1. Payment history
  2. Credit utilization
  3. Length of credit history
  4. Types of credit
  5. New credit
  6. Hard inquiries
  7. Amount of debt

Credit Score Categories

  • Excellent: 720+
  • Good: 690 to 719
  • Fair: 630 to 689
  • Bad: 629 and under

Managing Your Credit

  • Pay bills on time.
  • Keep credit card balances low.
  • Monitor your credit report regularly.
  • Avoid applying for too many credit accounts.
